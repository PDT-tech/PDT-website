# PDT Singers — Adding Events to the Website
## For Moss Egli, Social Media Manager

The Events page on the member portal shows upcoming sing-outs, social events, and High Council meetings. Everything on that page comes directly from the Calendar — there's no separate "Events blog" to manage anymore. One entry, one place.

---

## How to add a new event

1. Go to the **Calendar** page in the member portal
2. Click **+ New Event** (top right of the calendar)
3. Fill in the form:
   - **Event type** — choose Performance, Social Event, or Board Meeting
   - **Title** — e.g. "FIRST FRIDAY Sing-out" or "Summer Picnic"
   - **Date, Location,** and any time fields (call time, on-stage time for performances)
   - **Public description** — this is the only field that shows as narrative text on the Events page. Use it for anything members need to know: directions, what to wear, what to bring, a Google Meet link for HC meetings. Leave it blank if the structured fields say everything needed.
   - **Notes** — internal only, never shown publicly. Use this for operational reminders (parking instructions, contact name, etc.)
4. Click **Save**

The event appears on the Events page immediately — no separate publish step.

---

## How to edit an existing event

1. Go to the **Calendar** page
2. Click the event on the calendar to open its detail panel
3. Click **Edit**
4. Make your changes and click **Save**

The Events page reflects the update immediately.

---

## What shows up on the Events page

All upcoming performances, social events, and HC meetings appear automatically, sorted soonest first. Past events drop off on their own. If an event is marked cancelled, it shows with a CANCELLED notice until the date passes, then disappears.

Rehearsals never appear on the Events page.

---

## What you don't need to do anymore

There is no "+ New Event Post" button on the Events page. All event creation and editing happens on the Calendar. If you're on the Events page and need to add something, click **+ New Event** there — it takes you straight to the calendar form.
